Our Services

“Mental Health Research, Programs & Activities“

At Solving Lab Consultancy (SLC), we are dedicated to advancing mental health research, programs, and activities tailored specifically to the needs of children, adolescents, girls, women, and vulnerable populations. Our services in this realm are designed to address the unique challenges and vulnerabilities faced by these groups with empathy and expertise. For children and adolescents, we offer a range of research initiatives, programs, and activities aimed at promoting mental well-being and resilience during critical developmental stages. Our services include conducting research on topics such as childhood trauma, resilience-building interventions, and effective strategies for supporting young people's mental health. Additionally, we develop age-appropriate programs and activities, such as mindfulness workshops, art therapy sessions, and peer support groups, to empower young individuals with the skills and resources they need to navigate life’s challenges. In recognition of the specific mental health needs of girls and women, SLC offers targeted research and programming focused on issues such as gender-based violence, reproductive health, maternal mental health, and empowerment initiatives. Through our research efforts, we seek to identify barriers to mental health access and develop evidence-based interventions to address them. Our programs and activities for girls and women may include counseling services, leadership workshops, and advocacy campaigns aimed at promoting gender equality and mental well-being. Moreover, SLC is committed to serving vulnerable populations, including refugees, survivors of conflict, individuals experiencing homelessness, and those living in low-resource settings. Our services for vulnerable populations prioritize cultural sensitivity, trauma-informed care, and community engagement. We offer tailored research studies, programs, and activities aimed at addressing the intersecting social determinants of mental health and promoting resilience and recovery among marginalized groups. As part of our commitment to supporting mental health in crisis situations, SLC also provides Psychological First Aid (PFA) trainings to equip frontline responders and community members with the skills and knowledge needed to provide immediate support to individuals in distress. These trainings focus on principles of psychological first aid, trauma-informed care, and self-care strategies, empowering participants to effectively respond to mental health emergencies and promote psychosocial well-being in their communities. Whether you are seeking research expertise, program development support, or training in Psychological First Aid, SLC is here to partner with you in promoting mental health and resilience among children, adolescents, girls, women, and vulnerable populations. Together, we can work towards building a more compassionate and inclusive society where everyone has the opportunity to thrive emotionally and psychologically.
